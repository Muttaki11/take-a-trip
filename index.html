I don’t believe there is a “right” way to travel – everyone should be
free to roam around the world in the way they wish.

  • Don’t forget that all the cities you visit around the world are not your personal theme
    park, and have other interests than satisfy your needs or preferences.
  • Get to know the people, listen to them, don’t judge, be respectful, and leave
    whatever attitude you have at home.
  • Do you remember when you were a kid and would get totally absorbed in something?
    Reclaim that curiosity, that joy, and that attitude towards life.

However, the most memorable experiences usually happen when you stumble upon places that
you did not expect – or even planned – to see. And a benefit of organizing everything before
traveling lays also in the possibility to make time for totally spontaneous activities. By turning
left instead of right, you’ll discover places you didn’t even know existed.
